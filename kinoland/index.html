Project overview

The product

Kinoland is a chain of movie theaters in Almaty. They want to make the theater experience more convenient and enjoyable, starting from choosing the right movie on their website. Filters, detailed movie information, high-quality trailers, and reliable ratings/reviews will make the “picking film” process easier and faster.

Problem

Picking the right movie to watch in theaters might be challenging and time-consuming.

Goal

Design a website for the Kinoland movie theater chain, so their customers can spend less time and effort deciding what to watch.

User Research

I conducted interviews with 5 participants, which I then turned into empathy maps and personas to better understand the needs and pain points of users. It was clear that users go to theaters to relax and enjoy their time with friends or alone. Obviously, they prefer to find a good movie to watch. Some users take selecting movies more seriously than others, so they have to make research, check more detailed movie information (trailers, ratings, reviews, cast) on different websites. This decision process might be time-consuming and tiring but people don’t want to waste their money on movies they wouldn’t like.

Pain Points

1

Time

Users don’t want to spend too much time researching movies

2

Movie Choice

Picking films is challenging without enough movie information and reliable ratings. Also, necessary tools are required such as filters, search bar, and sorting.

3

Cost of the tickets

Movie tickets might be too expensive, especially for users with low budget

4

Accessibility

Most movie theaters' websites are not fully responsive (tablet, mobile screens).

Usability study: parameters

Study type:

Moderated usability study

Location:

Kazakhstan, United States, remote

Participants

5 participants

Length:

15-20 minutes

Usability study: findings

Two rounds of usability studies were conducted.

  • During the first usability study participants tested the low-fidelity prototype helping to identify some problems that needed to be fixed before moving to mockups.
  • Second usability study was conducted on a high-fidelity prototype, showing that the final design needed only minor refinements.

Round one findings

1

Most of the main features are intuitive and easy for all users

2

Users need to be able to sort the list by the highest ratings

3

Users need a search bar to look for a specific movie

Round two findings

1

Users need a more obvious appearance of a movie preview information

2

Users want to see the main cast in a movie preview section

Refining the design

Mockups

Based on the insights identified through the usability studies, there were some changes applied to the design.

First Usability Study

Problem № 1

Most of the users needed a search bar along with filters to find a specific movie.

Solution

That’s why the “filters” icon was replaced by a magnifying loop in the navigation bar.

Problem №2

Also, after the first usability study, it became apparent that in order to improve the searching experience, a sorting feature is required.

Solution

So users can sort movies by the highest ratings, by popularity, or by newest releases.

Second Usability Study

Problem

The second usability study pointed on minor problems, such as not obvious appearance of the movie preview information, and lack of cast information in the preview.

Solution

They were resolved by opening a separate modal view of preview info, and including a line of the main cast to it.

Accessibility considerations

1

Colors Contrast

The colors of the most important texts were tested on www.webaim.com. The contrast of the colors will be convenient for people with some visual impairments.

2

Responsive design

The web design is responsive to desktop, tablet, and mobile screens. I also kept in mind cases when a user needs to zoom in, so there won’t be any overlap between elements.

Takeaways

Impact:

Most of the feedback was positive, users never failed to complete any task, and described the overall experience as “easy” and “straightforward”.

Here’s a quote of one of the participants:

“It’s super easy. I wish more theaters followed this path”

What I learned:

  • I have learned the ways to manage space in different sizes (desktop, tablet, mobile).
  • Designing and animation in Adobe XD.
  • This project proved to me once again that simplicity is a priority. Whenever I even slightly overcomplicated features, some participants would notice it and struggle with that.
