Project overview

The product

Secret Garden is a new stylish restaurant in Almaty, Kazakhstan. It offers a variety of food options and excellent service. They are always happy to adjust the recipe for their customers because they believe that everyone deserves a perfect meal.

Problem

Many people with allergies or specific food preferences struggle to search for appropriate meals on the menu.

Goal

Design a brand new ordering app for the Secret Garden restaurant to help people make more conscious food decisions and find their perfect meals.

User Research

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users and their needs. A primary user group identified through the research was working adults who don’t have time to cook meals but want to stay conscious about what they eat for different reasons (health, religion, allergy).

Even though it might seem that being busy at work is the only reason why working adults don’t cook themselves, there could be many other factors, such as spending time with their families and friends. They prioritize their health by maintaining a work-life balance. That’s why a flexible food ordering service is so important. It enables busy people with food preferences/restrictions to quickly and easily find their perfect meal, so they have more free time for themselves and their close ones.

Pain Points

1

Time

Working adults are too busy spending time cooking meals

2

Food Choice

The searching mechanism is not as convenient and flexible as it can be. Choosing food can be challenging, especially if ordering something new

3

Delivery Time

Often the estimated delivery time is wrong, which might ruin the busy schedule of a working adult

4

Accessibility

Applications for ordering food lack assistive technologies, therefore excluding people with impairments. For example, small font size or the absence of audio descriptions

Usability study: findings

There were two rounds of usability studies:

  • The first study was conducted on a low-fidelity prototype helping to guide the design from wireframes to mockups.
  • The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and pointed out minor problems that should be fixed

Round one findings

1

Users want to customize existing meals from the menu

2

Users need more intuitive filters

3

Users want the contact info of a delivery person and restaurant

Round two findings

1

Users need a more intuitive checkout process

2

Users need more information about the ingredients, such as icons and price

3

Users need a more obvious filter icon

Problem

Second Usability Study

Problem

After the second usability study, it became apparent that the checkout flow had some inconveniences, such as the busyness of the screen, but most importantly - confusion on the order confirmation.

Solution

I divided the checkout process into smaller steps to make screens look less busy. Additionally, I added status trackers to make the process more intuitive and avoid unwanted confirmation.

Accessibility considerations

1

Food Choice

People with different food preferences/restrictions (allergies, diets, religion, beliefs etc.) can easily customize their meals and the whole menu.

2

Fonts/Icons with texts

The fonts of the text are easy-to-read. All important icons are accompanied with text, so people with visual impairments can use them too (with screenreader).

3

Contrast

The colors were tested on accessibility sites, such as webaim.com, and color-blindness.com. The contrast of the colors will be convenient for people with some visual impairments and colorblindness.

Takeaways

Impact:

The main intention of the Secret Garden app’s customization features was to make the ordering process convenient for people with some food restrictions. But it turned out that other groups of users also would like to be more conscious about their food choices.


“If you need halal food and you exclude bacon, this app will dominate everywhere!”

“They have diet section, that’s cool”

“I would like to be more conscious about what I eat, so I could add vegetables and exclude sugar”

What I learned:

During the course of creating this project, I learned a lot of valuable lessons about UX design in general.

The most crucial thing for me is the concept of putting users front and center in all phases of the design process. I was introduced to many tools that can contribute to the user-centered design (empathy maps, personas, user journeys, etc.). This mindset gave special meaning to the learning process.
